Delivery-stage confirmation alert

Prevent accidental, irreversible advancement of a PO's delivery/collection stage.

Objective

On the Purchase Order tracker, stages move forward only — once a stage is set it cannot be undone. A single mis-click currently commits the change with no warning. This enhancement adds a confirmation prompt before any stage is advanced.

Actors
Factory In-chargeSite SupervisorAdmin
Current behaviour

Clicking a future stage in the Delivery/Collection Status Tracker immediately records it. Completed stages lock; there is no "undo". No confirmation is shown.

Proposed behaviour
  • Clicking a not-yet-reached stage opens a confirmation dialog naming the target stage.
  • The dialog states clearly that the change cannot be reversed.
  • Confirm advances the stage (existing behaviour); Cancel makes no change.
  • The final stage ("Received/Collected") continues to route through the existing Receive goods flow, unchanged.

3

QS approval — sourcing & price gates

A QS signs off the buy in two steps — the sourcing before the PO, then the price on the PO — running in parallel with, and independent of, factory delivery.

Objective

Give the Quantity Surveyor (QS) control of the buy through two checkpoints: Gate 1 approves who we buy from (sourcing) before the PO is generated, and Gate 2 approves how much we pay (price) on the PO. The QS / price track is standalone — it never blocks, and is never blocked by, the factory (FIC) delivery track. This changes the Purchaser's flow only; no other role's flow changes.

Actors
PurchaserQSAdmin
Current behaviour

The Purchaser assigns a supplier to each buy line and clicks Generate Buy PO directly. The QS role exists but is view-only — it has no approval action. There is no QS sign-off before or after a PO is raised.

The two gates
  • Gate 1 — sourcing (before the PO): QS approves the items, suppliers and quantities. If the Purchaser has already entered prices, Gate 1 approves the sourcing + price together — no Gate 2 unless the price is later edited.
  • Gate 2 — price (on the PO): whenever the Purchaser enters or edits a price on the PO, it goes back to the QS for a price approval. Every price edit re-triggers Gate 2.
PO lifecycle after generation — two parallel tracks
The moment the PO is generated (once, after Gate 1) it is live: the supplier has the order and the FIC delivery track starts immediately and flows on its own. In parallel, the Purchaser can edit the price and get it QS-approved (Gate 2) as many times as needed. The PO therefore carries two independent statuses:
  • Delivery status (FIC): In transit → … → Delivered — the existing flow, unchanged.
  • Price status (Purchaser / QS): Awaiting pricing → Pending QS price approval → Price approved.
Neither track waits on the other. They meet only at Close: the Purchaser can Close the PO only when both (a) FIC has marked it Delivered and (b) the latest price is QS-approved (nothing pending). Close freezes the PO.

Proposed behaviour (Purchaser flow)
  1. Manager approves the PR → APPROVED (unchanged).
  2. Purchaser assigns suppliers and requests quotes (RFQ). Price is optional here — leave it blank or enter it now. Clicks Submit for QS approval → PENDING QS APPROVAL; Generate Buy PO locks.
  3. QS · Gate 1 reviews the sourcing (and the price, if one was entered) → QS Approve → QS APPROVED, Generate Buy PO unlocks. (or Send back with a reason → APPROVED.)
  4. Purchaser clicks Generate Buy PO → the PO goes live. FIC delivery starts immediately, in parallel.
  5. Purchaser enters or edits the unit price on the PO and clicks Submit price for QS approval → price status PENDING QS PRICE APPROVAL. (Skipped if the price was already approved at Gate 1 and not edited.)
  6. QS · Gate 2 reviews the price → QS Approve Price → price status PRICE APPROVED. Every later price edit returns to this gate.
  7. Purchaser clicks Close once FIC has marked Delivered and the latest price is QS-approved → CLOSED (frozen).
QS button label
The QS uses one Approve button whose label reflects what's on screen: QS Approve when there is no price to approve (sourcing only), QS Approve Price once a price is present (Gate 1 with a price, or any Gate 2). Base the switch on the PO / stage (or "every buy line has a price"), and record the gate in history as QS_APPROVE_SOURCING vs QS_APPROVE_PRICE so the trail is unambiguous.
Open question for client — still to confirm
  • Approval granularity: does QS approve per PR (the whole PR in one action) or per supplier (approve some, send others back individually)? This decides whether approval state lives on the PR / PO or on each supplier line — see TSD #3.
  • Can QS reject outright, or only send back to the Purchaser? (Assumed: send-back only.)
Acceptance criteria
  • Submitting a PR moves it to PENDING QS APPROVAL and locks Generate Buy PO; the price may be blank or filled at submit.
  • Gate 1: QS approves sourcing (plus the price, if one was entered) → QS APPROVED and Generate Buy PO unlocks. A price approved at Gate 1 needs no further approval unless edited.
  • Generating the PO makes it live and starts the FIC delivery track immediately; the QS / price track and the FIC track never block each other.
  • Entering or editing a price on the PO sets price status to PENDING QS PRICE APPROVAL; QS approve → PRICE APPROVED. Every price edit re-triggers this.
  • Close is allowed only when FIC = Delivered and the latest price is QS-approved; closing freezes the PO.
  • QS approval covers the buy side only; the stock portion still goes to the FIC independently. All submit / approve / send-back / close events appear in history.
  • No other role's flow changes.
4

Purchase Order price editing

Correct a mis-keyed unit price on an OPEN PO — a controlled, audited amendment.

Scope
This item is price editing only. Descriptions, quantities and the set of lines stay fixed after generation; only the unit price can be corrected. (Former enhancements #4 "PO editing" and #9 "Edit PO price after generation" are combined here.)
Objective

Today a PO's values are fixed once generated — only delivery details and lead-times can be edited, so a mis-keyed price forces cancelling and re-creating the PO. This enhancement lets an authorised user correct the unit price on a PO that is still in OPEN status, recalculating the total, as a tracked amendment with a full audit trail.

Actors
PurchaserAdmin
Current behaviour

PO detail supports editing delivery method/address, required date and shipment tracking. Line prices and the amount are read-only after generation. Corrections require cancelling and re-creating the PO.

Proposed behaviour
  • An Edit price mode on an OPEN PO makes each line's unit price editable. Description, quantity and the set of lines stay read-only.
  • The PO amount recalculates from quantity × edited price on save.
  • Tracked amendment — every price change is written to PO history (before → after), not a silent overwrite, with prices redacted for roles lacking price access.
  • If #3 (QS approval) is adopted, a price change re-triggers QS approval.
  • For a change after the supplier already holds the PO, issue a PO revision (Rev A → Rev B) rather than editing in place.
  • Editing is blocked once the PO is closed/received or cancelled.

  • Closed, received or cancelled POs cannot be edited.
5

Multiple suppliers per PR item (1, 1a, 1b, 1c)

Let one requested item be routed to several suppliers, each for a different processing purpose.

Objective

Allow a single PR item to be sent to multiple suppliers, shown as sub-lines under the parent item — item 1 with children 1a, 1b, 1c. In this business the same physical item is routed to different suppliers for different purposes (e.g. powder coating, polishing, anodising); each sub-line carries its own purpose, supplier, quantity and price, and each becomes its own PO.

Actors
DrafterPurchaserAdmin
Current behaviour

Each PR buy line maps to exactly one supplier. To involve a second supplier the item must be duplicated as a separate line. PR rows already group visually by line number (a stock/buy split shares one line), but there is no supplier sub-line concept.

Proposed behaviour
  • Under an item, the user can "+ Add supplier / purpose" to create sub-lines labelled 1a, 1b, 1c…
  • Each sub-line inherits the main item's description and carries its own purpose, supplier, quantity, remark and price, and can be quoted (RFQ) independently.
  • PO generation groups sub-lines by supplier as today — each supplier's sub-lines become that supplier's PO; the purpose is folded into the PO line description.
  • Sub-line labels (1a/1b/1c) appear consistently in the PR view, RFQ documents and PO references.

  • Each sub-line has its own purpose, supplier, quantity, remark and price, and can be quoted independently.
  • Sub-line labels and purposes render consistently in PR view, RFQ documents and PO references.
  • PO generation produces one PO per supplier from the relevant sub-lines.

PurchaserQSAdmin

GST 9% on the Purchase Order

Show subtotal, 9% GST and gross total on the PO and its PDF.

Objective

Purchase Orders currently show only a single total (the sum of line amounts). This enhancement adds Singapore GST at 9%, breaking the PO value into Subtotal → GST (9%) → Total in the detail view and PDF.

Actors
PurchaserQSManager
Current behaviour

The PO stores and displays a single amount (net of tax). No GST line exists on screen or on the PDF.

Proposed behaviour
  • PO totals show three lines: Subtotal, GST @ 9%, Total (incl. GST).
  • GST rate is configurable (default 9%) to survive future rate changes.
  • Applied on the PO detail, the PO PDF, and (optionally) the RFQ document.

Save Purchase Request as Draft

Let a Drafter save an unfinished PR and come back to it, without submitting for approval.

Objective

Today the PR form can only be submitted — there is no way to save a half-finished request. This enhancement adds a Save as Draft action so a Drafter can capture work in progress in a new DRAFT state and finish it later. A draft is not sent to the Manager and appears in no approval queue until the Drafter clicks Submit.

Actors
DrafterAdmin
Current behaviour

The PR form has a single Submit action that immediately moves the PR to PENDING for Manager approval. Closing the form without submitting loses everything entered.

Proposed behaviour
  • The PR form offers Save as Draft alongside Submit.
  • A draft stores all entered details — items, supplier sub-lines (1a/1b/1c), attachments — in a DRAFT status without notifying anyone.
  • Drafts are listed only for their creator (and Admin) under a Drafts filter, and can be reopened, edited or deleted.
  • Drafts never appear in the Manager's approval queue or other roles' views.
  • Submit on a draft moves it to PENDING — the normal flow from there is unchanged.

Purchase Order price editing

Extend the price-fill endpoint with an OPEN-PO price amendment + audit.

ScopePrice only. Edits po_items.unit_price and recomputes purchase_orders.amount. Description, quantity and the set of lines are not editable, so there is no add/remove-line logic.
Data modelNone new. Uses existing po_approvals.details (JSONB) for the before/after audit.
APIExtend the existing price-fill path PUT /purchase-orders/:poNo/prices (OPEN + BUY only): update line unit prices in a transaction, recompute amount, write a PO_PRICE_EDIT audit row with redactable price detail. Reject unless status = 'OPEN'. If #3 is adopted, clear QS approval on any change. (PUT /:poNo stays delivery/tracking-only.)
FilesBackend: routes/purchaseOrders.js, utils/auditTrail.js (redact price fields). Frontend: pages/PurchaseOrders.jsx (POView — editable unit-price cells, live total), lib/api.js (updatePOPrices), components/AuditTrail.jsx (PO_PRICE_EDIT label).
ConstraintsOPEN-only; STOCK POs excluded. Post-send changes go through a PO revision (policy), not an in-place edit.
Permissionsgenerate_po (or a dedicated edit_po_price if edit rights differ from creation).
EffortMedium — ~1.5–2 days incl. audit + test (narrower than full line editing; a price-fill endpoint already exists to extend).
5

Multiple suppliers per PR item (1, 1a, 1b, 1c)

PR line-model extension — sub-lines share a line_no, differ by suffix + purpose.

Data modelExtend pr_items with line_suffix TEXT DEFAULT '' ('' = main item, 'a'/'b'/'c'… = sub-lines) and purpose TEXT (free-text process label); the display label is line_no + line_suffix. Sub-lines share the parent's line_no + item_uid and each is a buy row with its own supplier / qty / remark / price. Idempotent ADD COLUMN IF NOT EXISTS migration + startup apply. Backward compatible: existing rows are line_suffix = ''.
APIroutes/purchaseRequests.js: create/update persist line_suffix + purpose and accept multiple buy rows per line_no; getPR orders by line_no, line_suffix, id. assign_supplier and RFQ (request-quote) already key on the row's supplier and so work per sub-line. generate-pos already groups by supplier_id|currency → one PO per supplier; the purpose is folded into the PO line description.
FilesBackend: routes/purchaseRequests.js (create/update/getPR/generate-pos), index.js (migration), db_schema.sql. Frontend: pages/PurchaseRequests.jsx — flattenItems/groupItemsForEdit to emit/read sub-lines, the PR form ("+ Add supplier / purpose" with purpose/supplier/qty/remark per sub-line), the PR view (render 1a/1b/1c + purpose), and the RFQ panel description; lib/rfqDoc.js labels.
RiskTouches the PR data model that reservation, RFQ and PO generation all read; regression-test the full PR→PO flow. Mitigated by keeping sub-lines as ordinary buy rows, so the assign panel, RFQ grouping and PO generation flow through unchanged.
PermissionsUnchanged (raise_pr, assign_supplier, generate_po).
EffortHigh — ~3–5 days (lower than a compare/award build, since sub-lines reuse the existing buy-row rails).

Supplier price-comparison table

Mostly a front-end view over multi-supplier data; couple with #5.

Data modelNone of its own — reads the per-supplier prices produced by #5 (pr_items sub-lines) and the RFQ quotes. A "chosen supplier" marker is a selected/award_status flag if "compare" mode is later added.
APIReuse GET /purchase-requests/:prNo (returns items with supplier + price). Selecting a winner reuses the assign_supplier update. No new endpoint strictly required.
FilesFrontend: pages/PurchaseRequests.jsx — a new comparison-matrix component in the assign panel (pivot items × suppliers, highlight min, select winner). Ties into the RFQ panel already added.

8

GST 9% on the PO

Rate field + subtotal/GST/total in view, PDF and generation.

Data modelAdd purchase_orders.gst_rate NUMERIC DEFAULT 9.0. Keep amount as the net subtotal (backward-compatible with existing dashboards/exports that sum it); derive gst = amount × gst_rate/100 and total = amount + gst at read/render time. (If the client wants gross stored, add gst_amount/total_amount columns instead — see FSD open question.)
APIgetPO returns gst_rate; compute gst/total in responses or on the client. Optionally allow zero-rating (e.g. non-SGD/overseas → gst_rate = 0) per the client's policy answer.
FilesBackend: routes/purchaseOrders.js / purchaseRequests.js (set default rate at generation), index.js (migration). Frontend: pages/PurchaseOrders.jsx (POView totals block), lib/poPdf.js (Subtotal / GST / Total rows in the table foot), optionally lib/rfqDoc.js.
Watch-outsCurrency: GST label should respect the PO currency symbol. Reconciliation: decide net-vs-gross once and apply consistently across dashboard, exports and finance module.
EffortMedium — ~2–3 days incl. PDF + test.
9

Save PR as Draft

One new status + a second save button; no new tables.

Data modelAdd DRAFT to the purchase_requests.status CHECK set. No new columns. Idempotent CHECK update + startup apply (index.js).
APICreate/patch accept status:'DRAFT' (a draft skips the SUBMIT audit + Manager notification). New/mapped POST /purchase-requests/:prNo/submit moves DRAFT → PENDING. GET /purchase-requests returns DRAFT rows only to their creator (or Admin). DELETE allowed while DRAFT.
FilesBackend: routes/purchaseRequests.js (create/update/list filter, submit), index.js (status CHECK). Frontend: pages/PurchaseRequests.jsx (Save-as-Draft button, Drafts filter tab), components/ui.jsx (DRAFT badge colour), lib/api.js (saveDraft / submitPR).
PermissionsReuses raise_pr. Draft visibility scoped to creator + Admin — no new permission.
EffortLow — ~1 day incl. test.
